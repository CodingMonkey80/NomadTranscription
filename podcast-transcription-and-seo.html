If you producing a podcast, or have anything to do with producing content on the internet, then you have likely become familiar with the term ‘SEO’.


SEO, which stands for search engine optimization, is a system of producing content that is likely to be found when a person searches certain keywords on Google, Yahoo or Bing.


This article is not going to teach you how to rank first on search engines overnight. But, while it is a continually changing science, there are a few things about SEO that are known truths:


Quality Content is King (or Queen)


Regularly updating relevant, quality content on your website is the best way to ensure that you work your way up the search engine rankings. So a regularly running podcast is a great thing. Most SEO professionals suggest that 2-6 time's a month is optimal.


Choosing the Right Keywords and Search Phrases is Important


Using the right keywords in your podcast, and in the title, can help to ensure that your intended audience is able to find your new content. It’s important to not fall for the trap of keyword cramming though. Search engines will penalise pages, whole websites even, that they deem spammy.


The Search Engine Spiders Don’t Have Ears


Unfortunately, even your greatest podcast is going to fail if your only audience is the search engine spiders. The pieces of code used to scour the internet for new content, called spiders, cannot decipher audio. Those spiders are responsible for determining which content will be best for the search engine user, you want to be sure that they enjoy your content.

They will pick up on those important keywords and that means that you have a better chance of ranking higher in the search results list.
